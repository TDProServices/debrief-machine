# The Debrief Machine ‚Äî AI Projects Edition v2.1

> A reusable, memory-preserving prompt designed to generate fully structured, project-specific briefings from AI conversations. Optimized for Claude Projects, ChatGPT custom instructions, and Perplexity Collections. Built for neurodivergent thinkers, AI experimenters, and anyone drowning in intelligent conversations.

## üß† What It Does

This prompt transforms complex AI conversations into structured, project-ready briefings complete with artifact tracking, prompt libraries, model performance notes, and reusable documentation. It's like having a personal AI conversation archivist that understands the nuances of working across multiple AI platforms.

## ü§ñ Platform-Specific Optimizations

### Claude Projects
- **Artifact Integration**: Captures all Claude artifacts with download links
- **Project Context**: Leverages Claude's project knowledge system
- **Conversation Threading**: Optimized for Claude's long-context conversations

### ChatGPT Spaces  
- **Memory Integration**: Works with ChatGPT's memory system
- **Custom GPT Documentation**: Tracks custom GPT creation and iteration
- **Plugin/Tool Usage**: Documents which plugins/tools were effective

### Perplexity Collections
- **Source Citation**: Captures all research sources and citations
- **Follow-up Question Tracking**: Documents research paths and dead ends
- **Collection Organization**: Maps how findings fit into existing collections

## üí¨ How to Use It

### For Claude Projects:
1. **Complete your conversation** in Claude
2. **Copy the full conversation** including artifacts
3. **Paste this prompt at the end**
4. **Save the output** to your project knowledge base

### For ChatGPT:
1. **End your conversation** with ChatGPT
2. **Use "Share Link" feature** to get full transcript
3. **Start new conversation** with this prompt + transcript
4. **Add to custom instructions** for recurring use

### For Perplexity:
1. **Complete your research session**
2. **Copy conversation including all sources**
3. **Use this prompt in new thread**
4. **Add to relevant Collection**
