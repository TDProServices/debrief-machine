## üî¨ **RESEARCH QUALITY ASSURANCE FRAMEWORK**

### **Source Authority Scoring (1-5 Scale)**

**Score 5 - Authoritative:**
- Peer-reviewed journals with high impact factor
- Government databases and official statistics
- Primary research from recognized institutions
- Expert testimony under oath
- Original scientific instruments/data

**Score 4 - Highly Credible:**
- Established news organizations with editorial standards
- Industry reports from recognized firms
- Academic books from university presses
- Professional association publications
- Verified expert interviews

**Score 3 - Generally Reliable:**
- Trade publications with editorial oversight
- Think tank reports with methodology disclosure
- Established magazines with fact-checking
- Corporate reports with audit verification
- Professional blog posts from verified experts

**Score 2 - Requires Verification:**
- News aggregators and secondary sources
- Social media posts from verified accounts
- Corporate marketing materials
- Opinion pieces from qualified authors
- Crowdsourced content with moderation

**Score 1 - Use with Extreme Caution:**
- Anonymous sources without verification
- Social media posts from unverified accounts
- Content farms and SEO-optimized articles
- Promotional materials without disclosure
- User-generated content without oversight

### **Bias Assessment Framework**

**Low Bias (Green):**
- Multiple perspectives presented
- Clear methodology disclosure
- Transparent funding sources
- Editorial independence verified
- Peer review or fact-checking evident

**Medium Bias (Yellow):**
- Clear editorial position disclosed
- Some methodological transparency
- Potential conflicts of interest noted
- Single perspective but well-supported
- Professional editorial standards

**High Bias (Red):**
- Strong advocacy position
- Limited methodological disclosure
- Significant conflicts of interest
- Emotional or inflammatory language
- Selective evidence presentation
